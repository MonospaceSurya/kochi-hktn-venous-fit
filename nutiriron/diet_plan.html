DIET PLAN

Raju, your diet plan is designed to support your healing process and manage your diabetes. We'll focus on nutrient-rich foods that promote wound healing and maintain healthy blood sugar levels.

Breakfast:

  • Option 1: 1 cup idli, 1/2 cup sambar, 1/4 cup coconut chutney (avoid peanuts in chutney).
  • Option 2: 1 cup oats porridge with 1/4 cup chopped fruits (apple, banana, guava), 1/4 cup nuts (almonds, walnuts).
  • Option 3: 2 egg omelette (prepared with olive oil), 1 cup mixed vegetable salad (avoid peanuts).

Lunch:

  • Option 1: 1 cup brown rice, 1/2 cup lentil stew (moong dal, masoor dal), 1 cup vegetable salad (avoid peanuts).
  • Option 2: 1 cup quinoa, 1 cup mixed vegetable stir-fry, 1/2 cup grilled fish or chicken.
  • Option 3: 2 vegetable roti, 1 cup mixed vegetable curry, 1 cup curd.

Evening Snacks:

  • Option 1: 1 cup buttermilk (lassi) with a handful of almonds.
  • Option 2: 1 cup fruit salad (avoid peanuts).
  • Option 3: 1 cup vegetable soup (avoid peanuts).

Dinner:

  • Option 1: 2 vegetable chapatis, 1 cup mixed vegetable curry, 1/2 cup curd.
  • Option 2: 1 cup brown rice, 1/2 cup chicken curry (prepared with minimal oil), 1 cup mixed vegetable salad (avoid peanuts).
  • Option 3: 1 cup vegetable broth with 1/2 cup grilled fish or chicken.

Fluids:

  • Drink 2-2.5 liters of water daily.
  • Include unsweetened herbal teas (like ginger, tulsi) throughout the day.

Remember:

  • Avoid processed foods, refined flour, and sugary snacks.
  • Prioritize whole grains and millets over refined grains.
  • Include 3 servings of fruits and 4-5 servings of vegetables daily.
  • Choose more green vegetables and dark-coloured vegetables over roots and tubers.
  • Include foods rich in omega 3 fatty acids (sardine, mackerel, walnuts, chia seeds, flax seeds, sunflower seeds).
  • Use spices like turmeric, ginger, garlic, and cinnamon for their anti-inflammatory properties.

ADDITIONAL TIPS

  • Diabetes: This diet plan is designed to manage your diabetes. Monitor your blood sugar levels regularly and adjust portion sizes accordingly. You can also consult with a registered dietitian for personalized diabetes management strategies.
  • Weight Loss: You have lost weight recently, which is good. Continue with your current physical activity and follow this diet plan to maintain a healthy weight.

BASELINE DIET PLANS

Class A (C0, C1):

  • Normal weight: 1800kcal diet with 60% carbohydrates, 25% fat, and 0.8g/kg body weight protein.
  • Overweight: 1500kcal diet with 55% carbohydrates, 20% fat, 0.8g/kg body weight protein, and 30g fiber.
  • Obese: 1200kcal diet with 55% carbohydrates, 15% fat, 0.8g/kg body weight protein, and 35g fiber.

Class B (C2, C3, C4):

  • Normal weight: 1800kcal diet with 60% carbohydrates, 25% fat, and 1-1.2g/kg body weight protein.
  • Overweight: 1500kcal diet with 55% carbohydrates, 20% fat, 1-1.2g/kg body weight protein, and 30g fiber.
  • Obese: 1200kcal diet with 55% carbohydrates, 15% fat, 1-1.2g/kg body weight protein, and 35g fiber.

Class C (C6):

  • Normal weight: 1800kcal diet with 60% carbohydrates, 25% fat, and 1.3-1.5g/kg body weight protein.
  • Overweight: 1500kcal diet with 55% carbohydrates, 20% fat, 1.3-1.5g/kg body weight protein, and 30g fiber.
  • Obese: 1200kcal diet with 55% carbohydrates, 15% fat, 1-1.2g/kg body weight protein, and 35g fiber.
